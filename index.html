Incoming Ph.D. Student in Machine Learning, Mohamed bin Zayed University of Artificial Intelligence (MBZUAI), Fall 2026

I am interested in effective computational paradigms for problems that are intractable yet matter: whether that means leveraging learning-based methods or quantum mechanics to make the previously impossible feasible. My current research focuses on GFlowNets and hierarchical reinforcement learning. I received my M.Sc. in Applied Physics (cum laude) from TU Delft and my B.Eng. in Nuclear Engineering from Fudan University.

Vision

I believe the most worthwhile work turns science fiction into reality, not for its own sake, but because the things that once seemed impossible tend to be the ones that most improve people's lives. Of course, that requires the efforts of social scientists and everyone else to ensure these technologies genuinely serve human flourishing rather than undermine it. Alongside that, I find myself genuinely curious about why the universe is so unreasonably well-described by mathematics, and more specifically, what mathematical structure our universe is isomorphic to.
