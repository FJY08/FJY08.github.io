My recent interests include measurement-free QEC schemes and fault-tolerant quantum computation. I received my M.Sc. in Applied Physics (cum laude) from TU Delft and my B.Eng. from Fudan University.

Research Experience

Shenzhen Institute for Quantum Science and Engineering Dec. 2024 – Jun. 2025
Research Intern — Shenzhen, CN

Developing a measurement-free quantum error correction scheme exploiting the Fermionic properties of electrons in spin qubit platforms. The feedback problem maps to a four-color linear separability problem on a hypercube.

QuTech, TU Delft Sep. 2023 – May 2024
Research Intern — Delft, NL

Studied biased noise in holographic quantum error correcting codes. Built the first automated tensor network operator push program; discovered a holographic code achieving a 50% maximum threshold under all pure Pauli errors, exceeding the hashing bound under high bias.

Fudan University — Institute of Modern Physics Jan. 2021 – Mar. 2022
Research Assistant — Shanghai, CN

Measured transition spectra of highly charged Ru ions using an Electron Beam Ion Trap (EBIT), achieving wavelength precision of 0.002 nm, contributing to Ru ion clock development and fine-structure constant studies.
